Interviews

To understand the experiences students have faced in various ride situations, we conducted nine user interviews with students from different university and college programs. Our questions focused on three main metrics

Insights

Interview Question Criteria


Students felt it was difficult to manage and communicate with riders?


“When a ride is scheduled, it may occasionally be moved earlier or later for various reasons. It is important to maintain open and friendly communication so the person can understand the situation and follow up with us accordingly.

Troubles locating flexible and budget friendly rides?


“With regular ride apps, the fare is fixed and non-negotiable, which can be unaffordable for students, especially international ones. However, when rides are provided by peers, there is more flexibility in pricing


Students have trust issues with raiders?


“People are often wary of taking rides from strangers, raising concerns about safety. How can the company ensure and justify the security and safety of the rides they provide? What measures are in place if a driver lacks a valid license and a proper driving history? .


Brainstorming

Takeaways

Being the listener

Conducting remote interviews really put me on the spot, but I was able to find my footing. I did my best to let conversations free-flow so I could gain unique insights from each of my interviews. Often I would get similar opinions but frustrations that varied. Each interview provided me with new perspectives that I could incorporate into our brain-storming.
